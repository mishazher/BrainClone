Memory: Beach Weekend with The Crew — May 17

After finals wrapped up, I finally got a real break. Dev organized a beach weekend at Ocean City and most of The Crew came along — Dev, Sarah, and Marcus all piled into Sarah's old Honda while I drove down with Maya. It was the first proper trip Maya and I have taken together since the Fraternity Formal, and it felt easy in a way that surprised me.

Saturday morning Marcus insisted on a sunrise run along the boardwalk, which turned into all of us getting breakfast burritos at a tiny place called Rosa's Cantina. Dev spent half of breakfast trying to convince me to join his summer side project — a small trading bot, obviously inspired by everything I told him about the QuantCo Program in Chicago. Sarah, ever the skeptic, kept poking holes in his plan, and honestly she was right about most of it.

In the evening we built a bonfire on the beach. Maya told stories about her internship and the FinCorp Terminal, and Marcus admitted he's thinking about transferring to a school closer to home, which got quiet fast — he's been the glue of The Crew since freshman year. We made a pact to do one big trip every summer no matter where everyone ends up, starting with a camping trip to Shenandoah in August.

Driving back Sunday night, sunburnt and tired, I realized this semester gave me more than an internship pipeline — it gave me people I don't want to lose.
